Know Before You Call

Common Airline Policy Guidance

General information about how major airlines typically handle common traveler requests. Policies vary — always confirm directly with the airline.

24-Hour Cancellation Rule

  • ✓ U.S. DOT requires airlines to allow 24-hour free cancellation on most tickets booked at least 7 days before departure
  • ✓ Applies to tickets purchased on the airline's own website or app
  • ✓ Refund goes back to original payment method within 7 business days
  • ✓ Third-party bookings may have different policies

Flight Change Policies

  • ✓ Many major airlines removed change fees on domestic main cabin and above tickets
  • ✓ Basic economy fares often cannot be changed after the 24-hour window
  • ✓ Same-day changes may be available as a paid option on some carriers
  • ✓ Fare difference may still apply on ticket changes

Refund Eligibility

  • ✓ Refundable tickets qualify for full refund to original payment
  • ✓ Non-refundable tickets typically receive airline credit, not cash
  • ✓ Airline-canceled flights generally entitle you to a full refund
  • ✓ Significant schedule changes may also trigger refund rights

Ticket Name Changes

  • ✓ Minor spelling corrections are usually allowed with a fee
  • ✓ Legal name changes (e.g., after marriage) accepted with documentation
  • ✓ Full passenger transfers are generally not permitted
  • ✓ TSA requires your name to match your government-issued ID

Baggage Allowances

  • ✓ Most U.S. airlines charge for checked bags on domestic routes
  • ✓ Carry-on allowances vary by fare class and airline
  • ✓ Credit card holders often receive a free checked bag benefit
  • ✓ Oversize and overweight fees apply above standard limits

Check-In & Boarding

  • ✓ Online check-in typically opens 24 hours before departure
  • ✓ Airport check-in closes 45–60 minutes before domestic departures
  • ✓ Mobile boarding passes accepted at most major airports
  • ✓ International flights require earlier airport arrival times
